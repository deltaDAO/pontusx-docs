# The EU Data Act 

## Practical guide to implementation: Ready to go in 24 hours. 
How do you get started?

### Phase 1: Regulatory & Strategic Assessment

- **Data inventory**: Identify which of your products fall under the Data Act. What data do they generate? Who is the “user”? 

- **IP Check**: Classify this data. Which parts constitute genuine trade secrets? Which parts are merely process data?

### Phase 2: Onboarding to Pontus-X

- **Registration**: Use [deltaDAO’s “Fast Track” onboarding](https://onboarding.delta-dao.com/) process. Verify your company’s identity. 

- **Service definition**: Describe your first data offering. Use templates to ensure interoperability. 

- **Publication**: Publish the offering in the Pontus-X catalogue. Decide: Download (for non-critical data) or Compute-to-Data (for IP-critical data)?

### Phase 3: Contract management and compliance

- **Access privileges**: Define who has access. Define conditions.

- **Contracting**: Review and approve contract templates (Data Use Agreements) or include your own terms.

### Phase 4: Go live

- **Publication successful**: The service can be found and consumed.

## Conclusion and outlook
The EU Data Act is a cornerstone of future European industrial policy. It enforces a level of openness that may initially seem culturally and technically counterintuitive to many European businesses. Yet the risks of isolation are now greater than the risks of openness. 

**Pontus-X is the necessary transaction layer to make the complex requirements of the Data Act (interoperability, access rights, IP protection) manageable.** Pontus-X your business a toolset to: 

- Increase legal certainty and ensure compliance. 

- Protect your most valuable asset, your know-how, even when data acess is granted.

- Develop new, scalable digital revenue models.
