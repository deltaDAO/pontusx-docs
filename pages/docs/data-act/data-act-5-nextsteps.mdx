# The EU Data Act 

## Practical guide to implementation: Ready to go in 24 hours. 
How do you get started?

### Phase 1: Regulatory & Strategic Assessment

-**Data inventory**: Identify which of your products fall under the Data Act. What data do they generate? Who is the “user”? 
-**IP Check:**: Classify this data. Which parts constitute genuine trade secrets? Which parts are merely process data? Document this in case of an official audit (Art. 4(8) notification).

### Phase 2: Onboarding to Pontus-X

-**Registration**: Use deltaDAO’s “Fast Track” onboarding process. Verify your company’s identity.
-**Service definition**: Describe your first data offering. Use templates for “Gaia-X Self-Descriptions” to ensure interoperability. 
-**Publication**: Publish the offering in the Pontus-X catalogue. Decide: Download (for non-critical data) or Compute-to-Data (for IP-critical data)?

## Conclusion and outlook
The EU Data Act is a cornerstone of future European industrial policy. It enforces a level of openness that may initially seem culturally and technically counterintuitive to many European businesses. Yet the risks of isolation are now greater than the risks of openness. 

Pontus-X is the necessary transaction layer to make the complex requirements of the Data Act (interoperability, access rights, IP protection) manageable. Pontus-X offers businesses a toolset to: 

- Ensure legal certainty (compliance). 

- Protect their most valuable asset – their own know-how – through Compute-to-Data, even when data is shared. 

- Develop new, scalable revenue models
